FAQ

Straight answers about AI agent architecture, custom agents, and what it actually takes to build one.

Hebridean AI answers common questions about architecture, pricing, timelines, and existing chatbot improvement.

What is AI agent architecture?

It's the process of designing and building an AI system that can autonomously perform specific tasks, with memory, persona, guardrails, and output quality engineered from the ground up.

Are you based in the Inland Empire?

Yes. Hebridean AI is based in the Inland Empire near Redlands and Yucaipa, California. I work with local businesses and can meet in person for consultations, audits, and ongoing support. I also work remotely with businesses nationwide.

Can you improve an existing chatbot?

Yes. Most businesses have some AI in place that's underperforming, the average chatbot fails on 45% of real customer tasks (Salesforce CRMArena, 2025). I audit what you have, identify where it's failing, and rebuild the architecture so it handles complex queries reliably instead of giving wrong answers or dead-ending.

What kinds of agents do you build?

Workflow automation agents, customer communication agents, compliance monitoring agents, research agents, report generation agents, and domain-specific operational agents.

How long does a build take?

Typical projects take 2 to 4 weeks from audit to deployment. Simpler engagements can be faster, multi-agent enterprise systems take longer. Each project starts with a paid discovery audit ($500–$1,000) that defines scope and timeline before build work begins.

Do you build from scratch or improve existing systems?

Both. Depending on the audit findings, the right move may be a new architecture built from scratch, or a targeted rebuild of an existing system's weak points. Most businesses already have some AI in place, the audit determines which path delivers better ROI.

What does it cost?

Hourly consulting is $125–$200/hr, monthly retainers are $1,500–$5,000/mo, and paid discovery audits are $500–$1,000. A free 30-minute discovery call is also available. Full details on the services page.

I already have an AI agent. Can you make it better?

Almost certainly. Most AI agents in production today achieve less than 55% task success (Salesforce CRMArena, 2025). That means nearly half of what your agent attempts fails silently. I audit existing systems, identify failure points, and rebuild the architecture so it actually works.

What happens if I deploy AI without proper architecture?

You join the 88% of AI projects that never reach production, or worse, you launch something that hallucinates, gives wrong answers, and erodes customer trust. A single hallucination incident can cost $18,000 to $2.4 million depending on your industry. The fix isn't a better model. It's better architecture.

How is this different from just using ChatGPT or a chatbot platform?

Off-the-shelf AI tools are built for general use, they hallucinate, fail on domain-specific tasks, and have no guardrails for your business context. 88% of AI projects fail because businesses skip the architecture step (Gartner, McKinsey). I build agents with validation gates, structured outputs, and error recovery, the difference between a prototype that works in demos and a system that works in production.
